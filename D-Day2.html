The Invasion



The invasion was supposed to happen June 5, 1944, but there was a storm that day, so the Allies could not attack. They could not attack because they could not land their
troops on the beach. The weather would not be good for a week though so they had to do something. They thought that if they waited, the Germans would catch on so
they went even when the weather was bad. Eisenhower said "O.K. lets go". There were thousands of armed fortifications along the beaches of Normandy. Eisenhower sent a
letter to every Allied soldier, sailor, and pilot. It was about how they should fight the battle with bravery and toughness. He also made another letter just in case
they failed. Right after midnight, June 6, 1944, Allied planes carrying pathfinders flew over Normandy. Pathfinders jobs are to place boxes on the ground that tell the
pilots of the planes carrying paratroopers where to drop the paratroopers, but this did not go as planed. German anti-aircraft guns started shooting at the planes. Since
the planes didn't want to get hit they flew higher and faster then they would usually. Since they were flying higher and faster than normal, when the 20,000 paratroopers
jumped out of the planes they went up to 20 miles off course. Fortunately, the paratroopers had a trick up their sleeves. They had dummy paratroopers. Fake paratroopers
that were wired with ammo so that when they hit the ground the ammo would go off. When it went off it made the Germans think they were being shot at. It also made it look
like there were more soldiers then there really were. The paratroopers started securing briges and roads when they landed.

The beach invasion was even bigger. The invasion on the beach had five different parts. Each part had a country that was assigned to it. Utah Beach-U.S.A., Omaha
Beach-U.S.A., Gold Beach-Britan, Juno Beach-Canada, and Sword Beach-Britan. In total there were 5,000 ships, 1,400 troop transport vessels, 805 cargo vessels, 138
war ships, and 221 escort vessles. 7,561 in total, that is a lot of ships. I'm only going to tell you about the two U.S. landings but if you want more info, feel
free to check out some other websites on your own. Utah went pretty smoothly, they got 23,000 troops and 1,800 vehicles on the beach, suffering only 197 casualties
(which later was known as the most successful amphibious invasion in American history). Most of the other landings worked out well too. The trick worked out perfectly,
the luftwaffe (the German air force) only had two planes. General Rommel was in Paris buying shoes for his wife's birthday and Hitler was asleep, his men were scared to wake
him up because of his bad temper. There where 2 invasions that did not go as planed. The first one was on Gold Beach so I am not going to really talk about that. The
other one though was on Omaha beach, but before we get into that, I first want to tell you that even though it seemed like the beaches were not really defended, they
were. They had 4 million land mines!!!, 8,000 permanent bunkers (which included machine-gun nests and artillery), 2,300 anti-tank guns, and 2,700 other guns. So
the Germans were somewhat prepared when the Allied troops were deployed. The Americans were deployed on a four-mile stretch of beach. The Germans had hundreds of
machine-gun nests above them, opening fire on the Americans. It was bad. Half of the first wave of American soldies died. In the end it was a success though, giving the
Allies a foot hold on occupied France.
