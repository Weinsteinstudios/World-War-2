The Battle of The Bulge, part two


How it ended


Long battles after long battles, things were geting harsh for the soldiers on each side. With supplies running low, the soldiers were extremly cold, some dying from frost bite.
There was not enough food, water or ammunition. Soldiers were burning the ammo and rifles that they had to keep warm. The fight still went on though. Finally, the weather
cleared up and the Allied air force could fight. The air force did supply drops and destroyed German tanks. Normally, German tanks could just drive away, but the harsh terrain
of the Ardennes made them get stuck, then, their crew would get out and abandon the tanks. With that, the Allies would win the battle.

Stuck German tank during the Battle of the Bulge.

Aftermath


The Battle of the Bulge was from December 16, 1944- January 16, 1945. It is considered the bloodiest battle of WW2. The U.S.A. had 19,000 dead, 47,500 wounded, and 23,000
missing or around 80,000 casualties. Germany had 100,000 casualties. Britain was not a big part with only 1,400 casualties. Just four months after the Battle of the Bulge ended,
the second World War ended.
