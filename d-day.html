The Story Behind D-Day

D-Day was a big part of World War 2. D-Day was a big operation. It had a lot of planning. That planning was not just about
charging the beaches, it was also about getting to that point. Now I am going to tell you about some of the things that
happend before charging the beaches. It all starts off with general George S. Patton. He was a respected General, by the
Allies and by Germany. He was respected because of his fighting ways. The Germans thought that if the Allies were going to
invade France they would put Patton in command. The Allies, knowing that, gave Patton a fake army called: The First US Army
Group. This fake army had troops and machines. They even had a fleet of planes. The thing is, it was fake. The planes were
made of wood, incapable of flying. The tanks and machines were just balloons painted to look like tanks and machines. They
put this "army" in Dover England so when the Germans flew over the base, they would see it. The Allies shot at the Germans to seem mad
so the Germans would think it was important . Then, in Spring 1944, Patton knew the Nazis were following him, so he went into a pub and
pretended to get drunk. There he said to a solider "Hey I guess I'll see you in Calais!". Calais is a French port located on the English Channel.
Now, the Nazis thought the Allies would invade Calais, so they put most of their equitment and troops there.

In the picture above, you can see that Calais is very close to Dover, England. That is why the Germans thought the Allies would

was called Operation Overlord which took place in Normandy, France. The reason why people say D-Day is because the day an invasion
takes place is referred to as D-Day.
